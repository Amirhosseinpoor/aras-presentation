Best-Response Dynamics

How agents update decisions over time

  • Asynchronous: Guaranteed convergence
  • Partial Synchrony: Almost sure convergence
  • Synchronous: Cycles and instability

Payoff Structure & Threshold Condition

\( \text{Payoff Matrix: } \begin{pmatrix} a_i & b_i \\ c_i & d_i \end{pmatrix} \)
\( n_A = deg_i - n_B \)
\( a_i n_A + b_i(deg_i - n_A) > c_i n_A + d_i(deg_i - n_A) \)
\( n_A (a_i - c_i + d_i - b_i) > deg_i (d_i - b_i) \)
\( n_A > \tau_i \cdot deg_i \quad \text{where} \quad \tau_i = \frac{\gamma_i}{\delta_i} = \frac{d_i - b_i}{a_i - c_i + d_i - b_i} \)

Lyapunov Potential Function

Anticoordinating Agents

\( \Phi_i(k) = \begin{cases} n_A(k) - \hat{n}_A & \text{if } x_i(k) = A \\ \hat{n}_A + 1 - n_A(k) & \text{if } x_i(k) = B \end{cases} \)

Coordinating Agents

\( \Phi_i(k) = \begin{cases} \check{n}_A - n_A(k) & \text{if } x_i(k) = A \\ n_A(k) - (\check{n}_A - 1) & \text{if } x_i(k) = B \end{cases} \)

Key Variables

\( n_A(k) \): Current number of A-neighbors.
\( \hat{n}_A \): The maximum number of A-neighbors an agent can tolerate before switching to B (tipping point).
\( \check{n}_A \): The minimum A-neighbors needed to keep playing A.

Threshold Normalization & "V-Block" Construction

The Transformation

Standardize any agent threshold to \( \tau = 1/2 \) by adding "dummy nodes".

  • Case \( \tau_i < 1/2 \): Add fixed B-neighbors to raise the threshold.
  • Case \( \tau_i > 1/2 \): Add fixed A-neighbors to lower the effort needed to switch.
\( m_i \approx |(1 - 2\tau_i) deg_i| \)

Key Variables

\( n_A(k) \): Current number of A-neighbors.
\( \hat{n}_A \): Maximum A-neighbors tolerated before switching to B (tipping point).
\( \check{n}_A \): Minimum A-neighbors needed to keep playing A.
