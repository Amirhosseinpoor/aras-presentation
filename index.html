Best-Response Dynamics: Synchrony, Asynchrony, and Partial Synchrony

Synchronous updates can lead to cycles, making them unsuitable for convergence. On the other hand:

  • Asynchronous Updates: Guarantee convergence to equilibrium, one agent updates at a time.
  • Partial Synchrony: Updates occur in small groups, leading to almost sure convergence in most cases.
  • Rejection of Synchronous Updates: They create cycles, preventing convergence.

Payoff Matrix and Threshold Equation

Payoff Matrix:

\( a_i n_A + b_i (deg_i - n_A) > c_i n_A + d_i (deg_i - n_A) \)

Grouping the terms gives the fundamental threshold equation:

\( n_A (a_i - c_i + d_i - b_i) > deg_i (d_i - b_i) \)

The fundamental threshold condition is:

\( n_A > \tau_i \cdot deg_i \) where \( \tau_i = \frac{\delta_i}{\gamma_i} \)

Lyapunov Potential Function

For Anticoordinating Agents (The Rebels):

\( \Phi_i(k) = \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} n_A(k) - \hat{n}_A & \text{if } x_i(k) = A \\ \hat{n}_A + 1 - n_A(k) & \text{if } x_i(k) = B \end{array} \right. \)

Where \( n_A(k) \) is the current number of A-neighbors, and \( \hat{n}_A \) is the maximum A-neighbors an agent can tolerate before switching.

For Coordinating Agents (The Followers):

\( \Phi_i(k) = \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} \hat{n}_A - n_A(k) & \text{if } x_i(k) = A \\ n_A(k) - (\hat{n}_A - 1) & \text{if } x_i(k) = B \end{array} \right. \)

Where \( \hat{n}_A \) is the minimum number of A-neighbors required to keep playing A.

Threshold Transformation: Dummy Nodes

Agents with varying thresholds are transformed to a uniform threshold of \( \tau = 1/2 \) by adding "dummy nodes."

  • Case \( \tau_i < 1/2 \): Add fixed B-neighbors to raise the threshold.
  • Case \( \tau_i > 1/2 \): Add fixed A-neighbors to lower the threshold.

The Magic Formula for the number of dummy blocks:

\( m_i \approx |(1 - 2\tau_i) \cdot deg_i| \)

Total Switches & Reaching Equilibrium

1

Upper Bound on Switches

  • The total number of strategy switches is limited to \( 6 |E| \), where \( |E| \) is the number of edges in the network.
2

Convergence Time

  • The system converges in linear time relative to the size of the network.
